\section{Abstract}
In geometry, Dini's surface is a surface with constant negative curvature that can be created by twisting a pseudosphere. It is named after Ulisse Dini(1845 â€“ 1918, Italia).

\section{Definition}
It is described by the following parametric equations:

\[
\begin{aligned}
x&=a\cos u\sin v \\
y&=a\sin u\sin v \\
z&=a\left(\cos v+\ln \tan {\frac {v}{2}}\right)+bu
\end{aligned}
\]
